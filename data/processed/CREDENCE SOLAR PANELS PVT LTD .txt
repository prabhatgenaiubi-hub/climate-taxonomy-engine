Brief Rating Rationales:

Crisil Ratings has reaffirmed its ‘Crisil BBB-/Stable/Crisil A3’ ratings to the bank facilities of
Credence Solar Panels Private Limited (CSPPL). The ratings reflect favourable demand outlook for
the solar industry, extensive experience of the promoters and healthy debt protection metrics.
These strengths are partiaily offset by susceptibility to increasing competition, volatility in raw
material prices and regulatory changes, large working capital requirement and exposure to project
risks.

Key Rating Drivers & Detailed Description

Strengths:

Favourable demand outlook for the solar industry: The company will benefit from the growing
emphasis on solar power in India and the long-term plans of the government to increase generation
from renewable sources, Introduction of protectionist measures by the government, such as basic
customs duty (BCD) of 40% and 25% on imported solar modules and solar cells, respectively, from
April 2022, and implementation of Approved List of Models and Manufacturers (ALMM) as well as
incentives for domestic players under the production-linked incentive (PLI) scheme increases the
cost competitiveness of domestic modules. Government-approved schemes such as Kisan Urja
Suraksha Utthan Mahabhiyan, Central Public Sector Undertaking, PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana
and rooftop scheme aiso drive demand.

Extensive industry experience of the promoters: The promoters have experience of 25 years in
the solar photovoltaic (PV) module manufacturing industry. This has given them an understanding
of the market dynamics and enabled them to establish relationships with suppliers and customers.
The company has solar PV module manufacturing capacity of 600 MW. Revenue growth over the
medium term will be driven by strong domestic demand.

Healthy debt protection metrics: The debt protection metrics were comfortable despite leverage
owing to moderately healthy profitability. Interest coverage and net cash accrual to total debt ;
ratios were 8.23 times and 0.49 time, respectively, in fiscal 2025 and are expected at similar :
levels over the medium term. F

Susceptibility to increasing competition, volatility in raw material prices and regulatory
changes: Operating margin remains vulnerable to sharp fluctuations in raw material prices (raw
material cost accounts for 70-88% of the operating income). For instance, prices of key inputs such
as solar cells dipped significantly in fiscal 2024 and in the first half of fiscal 2025. The company is
exposed to increasing competition from domestic as well as imported modules on account of large
capacity additions (and planned) in the domestic market with various levels of integration.

Indian manufacturers also face competition from Chinese imports. Module and cell prices reduced
significantly in fiscals 2024 and 2025 owing to supply glut in China amid restrictions imposed by the
US on Chinese imports. However, integrated operations with enhancement of module capacities
and implementation of BCD will enable CSPPL to be cost-competitive against Chinese imports. That
said, the risk of significant price reduction by Chinese players to lessen the impact of BCD persists.
Additionally, implementation of ALMM, which is expected to boost demand for domestic modules,
will remain monitorable as it can boost CSPPL’s growth.

Prices of key raw materials such as polysilicon, aluminium and copper, and solar cells have been
volatile. Though the company has price variation clauses for most raw materials and undertakes
order-backed procurement to mitigate this risk, any sharp rise in input cost may impact demand in
the domestic industry. CSPPL has an established market presence in the module business, which
helps the operating margin and enables it to cater to domestic content requirement module
tenders.

Growth remains vulnerable to changes in government policies. However, the central government's
focus on achieving a target of ~280 GW from solar power by 2030 is expected to support the
company.

Large working capital requirement: Operations are working capital intensive, as reflected in gross
current assets of 163.7 days as on March 31, 2025. The working capital cycle remains stretched
owing to large receivables and inventory.

Exposure to project risks: CSPPL is expanding its capacity and scheduled to commercialise its
project from July 2025. Demand risk is expected to be moderate as the industry is highly
fragmented owing to low entry barriers of small capital and technological requirements. Timely
- completion and successful stabilisation of operations at the new unit will remain key rating
sensitivity factors.

3.1. Background of the Company & Group Structure:

M/s Credence Solar Panels Pvt. Ltd. (CSPPL) was incorporated on 27-04-2021. CSPPL is promoted
and managed by Mr. Ashvinkumar Chakubhai Boda and Mr. Umeshbhai Chakubhai Boda. Credence
Solar Panels Pvt Ltd is in business of Manufacturing of high efficiency PV modules using monofacial,
bifacial, PERC and TOPCon technology. The company is situated at Rajkot, Gujarat being close to
major solar hubs like Bhuj and Kutch, benefiting from low logistic cost to large solar projects.

The Company is having an automated module manufacturing facility in Rajkot with an existing
capacity of 600 MW and have recently added a new production line of 1.6 GW scaling up total
capacity to 2.2 GW & the company has achieved a DCCO in the month of September 2025.

CSPPL. is the 1** Indian Company to commercially producing G12 Modules (G12 solar modules use
larger 210 mm wafers, offering higher power output and efficiency than smaller modules, making
them ideal for large-scale solar projects).

The promoters have experience of more than 25 years in the manufacturing industry of Electrical
switchboards, PCC, MCC and PLC Panels. The promoter has promoted M/s Deepjyot Engineers Pvt
Ltd, M/s Ascent Engineers & various other companies which is into manufacturing of Electrical
switchboards and Panels since 2013.
